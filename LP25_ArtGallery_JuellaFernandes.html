Waterfalls are natural features where water flows over a vertical drop or a series of drops in the course of a stream or river. They are often characterized by their beauty, power, and the mesmerizing sound of cascading water.Waterfalls are typically formed when a river or stream encounters a change in the landscape, such as a cliff or a steep descent. The force of gravity causes the water to plunge over the edge, creating a waterfall. Types of Waterfalls: Vertical Waterfalls: The water drops vertically, forming a clear and distinct fall. Horsetail Waterfalls: The water maintains contact with the rock as it falls, resembling a horse's tail. Plunge Waterfalls: Water descends vertically without maintaining contact with the underlying surface. Cataracts: Powerful, large waterfalls with a series of drops.Popular Waterfalls: Niagara Falls: Located on the border between the United States and Canada, Niagara Falls is one of the most famous and powerful waterfalls in the world. Victoria Falls: Found on the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe, Victoria Falls is one of the largest waterfalls in terms of width and height. Angel Falls: Situated in Venezuela, Angel Falls is the world's highest uninterrupted waterfall. Many waterfalls attract tourists, offering opportunities for sightseeing, hiking, and photography. Some waterfalls also provide a backdrop for recreational activities.
